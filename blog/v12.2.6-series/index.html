Architecture series

How your agent gets a second brain

Imagine two colleagues. One handles the front desk — talking to users, running tools, assembling answers. That's OpenClaw. The other works in a back office, studying every conversation, building a map of what the agent knows, and training a guide that picks the right knowledge for each question. That's OpenClawBrain.

The usual approach (RAG) is like a keyword search: find the closest-sounding text chunks and hope they help. OpenClawBrain replaces that with a guide who walks a map of connected knowledge, learns from corrections, and gets sharper over time. Each post in this series goes deep on one piece. Start wherever you like.

In benchmarks, the trained guide answers 97.5% of queries correctly vs. 89.6% for the best RAG baseline on recorded sessions. Under sparse feedback it widens to 92.0% vs. 67.1%, using about 5x less context per query. Full evidence: /proof/.
