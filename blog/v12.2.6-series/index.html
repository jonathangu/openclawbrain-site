OpenClawBrain v12.2.6+

This series explains how OpenClaw and OpenClawBrain divide the work, and why that split matters. OpenClaw handles the live runtime — sessions, tools, prompts, answers. OpenClawBrain handles learning — watching real conversations, training a routing function (route_fn) over a knowledge graph, and packaging the result into versioned brain packs that sharpen the agent over time.

The core idea: instead of dumping the nearest text chunks into the prompt (the standard RAG approach), a learned router walks a graph of structured relationships, picks the right context for each query, and improves from corrections and feedback. Each post goes deep on one piece. Start wherever you like.

Headline numbers: on recorded-session benchmarks the learned router (full_brain) answers 97.5% of queries correctly vs. 89.6% for the best RAG baseline — roughly 1 in 13 queries where RAG fails, the learned router gets right. Under sparse feedback it widens to 92.0% vs. 67.1%, using about 5x less context per query and winning 9 of 10 random seeds head-to-head. These are benchmark results, not live-traffic claims; see /proof/ for the full evidence ladder and artifact links.
