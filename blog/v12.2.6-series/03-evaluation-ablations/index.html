Post 3

Evaluation: prove it without pretending

March 2026 | v12.2.6+ series

OpenClawBrain needs a cleaner evidence story than "the simulations look good." Simulations matter, but they only prove the mechanism. Product claims need a stronger ladder.

Proven now

Deterministic simulations, local ablations, and reproducible figure generation for the routing mechanism and its update path.

Needs head-to-head

Recorded-session comparisons, shadow traffic, and narrow online rollout before claiming superiority on real OpenClaw workflows.

Not claimed

No blanket benchmark wins, no universal cost cuts, and no production superiority from simulation artifacts alone.

What simulations are for

Simulations are still useful. They tell you whether the learned route policy, the confidence gate, and the update rule behave coherently under controlled conditions.

The stronger proof path

StageWhat it provesWhat artifacts are required
Deterministic simulations The mechanism behaves coherently. Exact commands, seeds, CSVs, generated figures.
Offline recorded-session eval The same workload can be scored head-to-head across routing modes. Query set, scoring rubric, eval JSON, commit SHA.
Shadow traffic The brain helps on real OpenClaw traffic without being the only live path yet. Mirrored traffic slice, side-by-side metrics, retained outputs.
Narrow online rollout Operational value under real usage constraints. Success, correction rate, prompt size, latency, cost.

Reporting contract

  1. Every nontrivial claim needs a command, an artifact path, and a commit SHA.
  2. If an artifact does not exist yet, the table cell stays TBD.
  3. Do not summarize simulations as product wins on OpenClaw traffic.

The canonical instructions live in docs/reproduce-eval.md.
