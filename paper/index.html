The full write-up

Paper

by Jonathan Gu

Most retrieval systems work like a search engine — match keywords, return whatever looks similar, cross your fingers. This paper makes the case for a different approach: a guide that follows the connections between facts, learns from corrections, and sends less context for better results. Think of a librarian who knows the deploy checklist depends on the rollback policy — and remembers which shelf solved your last question.

What the paper covers

Four main ideas, each grounded in a different layer of evidence.

Clean split

OpenClaw serves; OpenClawBrain learns

OpenClaw serves answers from a frozen knowledge snapshot (an immutable bundle of context and routing weights). Off the live path, the learner builds fresher snapshots; promotion switches to the new one. This split means rollback is instant and learning never blocks a live response — like swapping a new edition of a reference book onto the shelf without closing the library.

Two signals, blended

Map knowledge + question fit

The guide blends two signals: what the knowledge map already knows about how facts relate to each other, and how well each fact fits the current question. A confidence gate shifts weight between them — lean on the question match when it's clear, fall back to map structure when it's ambiguous. Think of it like a librarian who checks both the card catalog and the question itself.

One learning rule

All feedback feeds into one update

Human corrections, outcome signals, automated labels, and teacher-model feedback all feed into a single learning rule. Human corrections carry the most weight — if a person says the guide picked wrong, that signal wins. It's like a librarian who listens most carefully to the patron who says "that wasn't the right book."

Future direction

A living map (not yet shipped)

The paper frames map maintenance (connecting, splitting, merging, and pruning knowledge) as a control problem. Today, the map's structure is set at build time. The target is a map that reshapes itself during live serving. See CLAIMS.md for the boundary.
